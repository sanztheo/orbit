## Competitor Findings

### Folk.app

- **Price:** $20/seat/mo (Standard), $40–60/seat/mo (Premium)
- **Position:** Most founder-targeted new entrant. LinkedIn-first contact rolodex with AI enrichment.
- **Strengths:** Clean UI, fast LinkedIn enrichment, magic fields for custom data, good email sequencing on Premium.
- **Fatal weaknesses:**
  - No mobile app — single most common complaint across G2, Capterra, Trustpilot (2025–2026)
  - Deal management paywalled behind Premium ($60/seat)
  - Duplicate detection only catches exact name matches
  - Reporting shallow even after Dec 2025 dashboard launch — no forecasting
  - No product backlog, no investor-specific pipeline
  - Enrichment capped at 500 contacts/month on Standard
  - Folk is a Rolodex, not an operating system. Solo founders hit ceiling in 3 months.
- **Sources:**
  - https://getdex.com/blog/folk-crm-review/
  - https://prospeo.io/s/folk-alternatives
  - https://www.coffee.ai/articles/attio-vs-folk-crm-startups/

---

### Attio

- **Price:** Free (3 seats) / $29/seat/mo (Plus) / $69/seat/mo (Pro) / Enterprise
- **Position:** "Notion for CRM" — flexible data model, strong free tier for solo use.
- **Strengths:** Extremely flexible data model, good email/calendar sync, strong free tier to hook founders.
- **Fatal weaknesses:**
  - Flexibility is a trap for time-poor founders — setup burden prohibitive without ops resources
  - No SLA — multiple reviewers documented direct lead loss during downtime with zero recourse
  - Automation credit caps on Plus plan
  - Native integrations missing: LinkedIn, Mailchimp two-way, Amplitude
  - AI features don't match depth promised
  - Explicitly went upmarket toward Salesforce in 2025, abandoning solo founder segment
  - Free tier hooks you, then $29/seat pain hits when you need basic automation
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.coffee.ai/articles/attio-vs-folk-crm-startups/
  - https://getcoherence.io/blog/solo-founder-crm-comparison-guide-2026

### Clay.com

- **Price:** $149–$800+/month (credit-based)
- **Position:** Data enrichment powerhouse, 100+ data providers, waterfall enrichment.
- **Strengths:** Unmatched data enrichment depth, 100+ providers, sophisticated waterfall logic.
- **Fatal weaknesses:**
  - Credit-based pricing burns budget catastrophically
  - One G2 reviewer consumed entire monthly credit allocation enriching 500 contacts in a single afternoon
  - Steep learning curve
  - Does not handle outreach natively
  - Clay is a data pipeline tool, not a relationship manager
  - Solo founders buy it hoping for magic, get a complex ETL workflow they can't maintain
- **Sources:**
  - https://www.coffee.ai/articles/attio-vs-folk-crm-startups/
  - https://prospeo.io/s/folk-alternatives

### HubSpot

- **Price:** Free / $20+/seat/mo (Starter) / Enterprise tiers
- **Position:** Market default. 62% SMB CRM market share. Everyone knows it.
- **Strengths:** Massive ecosystem, name recognition, extensive integrations, marketing automation breadth.
- **Fatal weaknesses:**
  - Free tier gutted September 2024 — new accounts capped at 1,000 contacts (down from 1 million), 2-user cap
  - Any automation, custom reporting, or sequences requires paid tiers
  - Founders report cost increases of 2,300% as they scale
  - Bait-and-switch model: land free, extract maximum revenue as you grow
  - Enterprise DNA visible in every UX decision — not built for one person
- **Sources:**
  - https://addtocrm.com/tools/best-crm-for-solopreneurs
  - https://fluidcrm.io/blog/crm-for-solo-founders/
  - https://getcoherence.io/blog/solo-founder-crm-comparison-guide-2026

### Pipedrive

- **Price:** $14–$99/seat/month
- **Position:** Sales-team pipeline tool, visual Kanban deals.
- **Strengths:** Clean Kanban UI, good for pure sales pipeline tracking, extensive app marketplace.
- **Fatal weaknesses:**
  - Built for SDR teams, not solo founders
  - "Missing features" appeared 231 times in negative 2025 reviews — mostly about reporting depth
  - Add-ons for everything you actually need (scheduler, automations, AI) paywalled on top of base price
  - Workflow automation breaks with conditional logic
  - Pricing restructured late 2025 with surprise price increases on renewal
  - Solves wrong problem: optimizes sales *team* pipeline, not solo founder managing clients + investors + prospects + product simultaneously
- **Sources:**
  - https://getcoherence.io/blog/solo-founder-crm-comparison-guide-2026
  - https://www.coffee.ai/articles/attio-vs-folk-crm-startups/

### Honorable Mentions

**Streak:**
- Eliminated free tier 2024–25
- Gmail-only, slows as data grows, no mobile CRM
- Best-case for freelancers, not founders

**Twenty (OSS):**
- No marketing automation, basic reporting, no mobile native app
- Requires self-hosting investment
- Strong for technical founders who want full data control but zero help with email AI or deal intelligence
- Source: https://getcoherence.io/blog/solo-founder-crm-comparison-guide-2026

**Less Annoying CRM ($15/seat):**
- Genuinely good, simple, beloved
- Zero AI, limited integrations, no investor pipeline, no backlog
- Built for 2009, priced for 2009
- The one incumbent whose philosophy (one flat price, no nonsense) Orbit should borrow

**Notion CRM templates:**
- What founders use before real tools
- Breaks at scale, no automation, no email sync, no enrichment
- The "before" state Orbit should replace
- Source: https://editorialge.com/crm-for-solopreneurs

## Key Insights Driving Product Decisions

### Insight 1: The 4-relationship-type problem is structurally unsolved
Solo founders juggle customers/prospects (CRM), investors (Affinity/Notion), partners/contractors (email), and job candidates (manual) — in 4 different tools. Zero tools unify these into one person-centric view. **Decision: Orbit's contact model tags relationship type on a single record, not separate databases.**

### Insight 2: Contextual AI follow-up is the #1 unrealized feature
"I had a call with this investor 3 weeks ago — here's the email AI drafted based on that call + their portfolio + what I said I'd follow up on." No tool does this. All "AI email" is generic GPT wrappers disconnected from relationship history. **Decision: Orbit's AI reads actual email thread + contact history before drafting. Not generic templates.**

### Insight 3: CRM-to-backlog link is a structurally unsolved problem
Insight from a customer call should flow into the backlog — tagged, prioritized, attributed. CRM vendors don't build backlog tools. Backlog tools don't build CRMs. This gap is 100% structural, not a missing feature. **Decision: native backlog in Orbit with feature-request-to-contact attribution. Not a Zapier integration.**

### Insight 4: Per-seat pricing is the real enemy
Only 50% of sub-10-person companies use a CRM. The other 50% use spreadsheets not because they're lazy, but because per-seat SaaS pricing is irrational for a solo operator. **Decision: Orbit prices per workspace, not per seat. One flat price.**

### Insight 5: 60-90 day CRM abandonment is the adoption pattern to break
Founders adopt a CRM, use it for 2-3 months, then abandon it because maintenance cost exceeds value. The tool that auto-captures relationship signals from email, LinkedIn, and calendar with zero manual input wins. **Decision: email sync is P0, not P1. Zero-manual-input is the design constraint, not a feature.**

### Insight 6: The emotional wound is identity dissonance
The deeper pain is not "I can't find a contact" — it is feeling like a data entry clerk instead of a founder. The ambient dread of knowing the CRM is out of date. **Decision: Orbit's UX never shows empty states as failures; it surfaces what's at risk (stalling deals, overdue follow-ups) as a proactive system, not a guilt machine.**

### Insight 7: Incumbents have abandoned the segment
Attio explicitly went upmarket. Folk hit its ceiling. HubSpot gutted its free tier. No one is left serving the solo founder. The migration window is open and there is no incumbent defending this position. **Decision: launch targeting Folk and Notion-template refugees specifically.**

## Dominant Current Patchwork (What Orbit Replaces)

Almost universal stack:
- Gmail/Outlook — primary comms hub (inbox doubles as CRM)
- Notion — investor pipeline, product backlog, docs, sometimes contacts
- Google Sheets / Airtable — deal tracking, fundraising stage, investor list
- Linear — engineering backlog (separate silo)
- Zapier/Make — glue between above tools when they break down
- Calendar (Google/Calendly) — meeting context, disconnected from everything else

The stack collapses at ~10–15 simultaneous conversations. Nothing talks to anything. Context lives in the founder's head.

## Willingness to Pay Signals

- Existing tools (Folk $20-40/user/mo, Attio paid tiers) actively used despite complaints — price sensitivity is moderate, not extreme
- Clear ceiling: ~$50/month solo. Above that, founders start looking for alternatives or revert to spreadsheets.
- Investors in one quote cited $700–$1k/year as "ridiculously expensive" — creates $200–$500/year sweet spot
- Lifetime deal appetite high (AppSumo mentioned repeatedly)
- Pay triggers: saves 2+ hours/week, prevents one missed deal, eliminates one existing subscription
- Solo founders compare tool cost against cost of a missed deal — one dropped investor follow-up justifies months of fees immediately

## Open Questions to Validate

1. **Email sync depth:** Does OAuth Gmail read-only suffice for auto-logging, or do founders expect Orbit to send email from within the tool? (Determines whether Resend is sufficient or SMTP integration needed on MVP)

2. **Investor pipeline vs customer pipeline:** Are these the same Kanban component with different stage labels, or do investors require structurally different fields (check size, firm, portfolio, lead/follow) that warrant a separate view?

3. **AI follow-up model:** Do founders want a full draft (click to send), or a summary + bullet points of what to cover? Full draft risks sounding robotic; bullets require more founder effort. Which increases adoption?

4. **Backlog complexity:** Do founders need a sprint system (sprints, story points, velocity) or just a prioritized list with a "this week" bucket? Over-engineering backlog kills the CRM-user adoption of that feature.

5. **Import format:** What format is the Notion CRM template exported as? Does Folk export cleanly to CSV? (Determines onboarding friction from top competitors.)

6. **Mobile:** Is a PWA sufficient (saves to home screen, offline-capable) or do iOS/Android native apps need to ship within 6 months to avoid the Folk complaint pattern?

7. **Multi-workspace:** Do solo founders manage multiple ventures? Would they pay for workspace isolation per project vs unified view?

8. **LinkedIn enrichment:** Can we scrape public LinkedIn data legally for enrichment, or must we use a partner (RocketReach, Apollo, PDL)? Cost implications for free tier.

9. **AI action limits:** Is 100 AI actions/month sufficient for a Solo tier, or does the follow-up drafting use case exhaust this faster than modeled?

10. **Team collaboration:** Is the 1–3 seat limit a feature (focused positioning) or a liability (founders want to add a VA or co-founder immediately)?
